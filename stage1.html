You have a Stage 1 of Brain Tumor

Overview :

Grade 1 tumors are benign (non-cancerous) and very slow-growing. Cells appear mostly normal under a microscope, and they are unlikely to spread into surrounding brain tissue, leading to the best prognosis.

Treatment:

  • Surgery: Often the only treatment needed, especially if the tumor can be completely removed.
  • Monitoring: If complete removal is not possible, doctors may monitor the tumor with regular imaging scans. If the tumor begins to grow, treatment may then become necessary.
